The History of The Antanov 225 Mriya

Specs

Antanov 225 Mriya Carring Soviet Space Shuttle

Development on the Antonov began in 1984, in Ukraine. Serving the purpose of transporting large componets for the Soviet Space program as well as acting as a areial launch pad for Soviet Space crafts during the Cold War.

Boeing 747 Carring American Space Shuttle

However the Americans where the first to successfull use large planes to transport space crafts and launch them from the air.

The Americans converted Boeing 747 into aircrafts able to cary the wait of a space craft by reducing wieght on the planes, strengthing the fuselarge and adding additional stabilizers to the aircrafts.

Antanov 124

Before the 225 was developed, the largest plane currently in the world, The Antanov 124.

Despite the enourmous size of the Antanov 124, it was just not able to fulfil the needs of the Soviet Space Program

Soviet Engineers did attempt to use the Antanov 124, but the plane had to under go a major redesign procees.

    Changes to the Antanov 124
  • Lengthen the fuselarge
  • Increasing the amount of wheels to 32, inorder to spread the load more evenly.
  • Adding root extensions the wings in order to increase the wing span.
  • The adding of an addtional two engines
  • Completly changing the tail, from a convetional tails to an H-tail, due to larger componets obstructing the convertional tail.

These changes then lead to the creation of the Antonov 225.

Collapse of the Soviet Union 1991

After the collapse of the Soveit Union, Ukraine's Soviet rule ended and Antonov Airlines became an Ukrainian company. Which still to this day operates in the oversized cargo market.

The Antonov 225 enter comercial service in 2001

The Antanov Today

Despite the age of the Antonov 225, it servers as the largest cargo plane in the world. Transporting cargo throught the world, which includes electrical transformers, transporting of plane fuselarges and assiting in humanitarian operations.

Antonov Airlines operates their fleet of Antonov, including the 225 out of the Boryspil International Airport in Kiev, Ukraine.
